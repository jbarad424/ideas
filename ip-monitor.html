The Problem

We Were Doing This by Hand

We've filed dozens of infringement complaints through Amazon by hand over the years. Each one meant searching Amazon, scrolling through hundreds of results, eyeballing each listing, and filling out complaint forms. It took 15 to 30 minutes per complaint, and we were still missing knockoffs that popped up between searches.

Some sellers started putting "NOT Wearable" in their titles to avoid showing up in our searches, even though their product clearly fits on a wrist. We needed something that checks every day and can't be tricked by a misleading title.

The Solution

A Daily AI Scan That Runs Itself

Every morning, automatically, no one has to touch anything:

🔍

Search Amazon's Entire Catalog

16 keyword searches across both patents. Handles pagination, rate limiting, and remembers everything it's already seen.

170+ listings found per scan
⚡

AI Quick Screen

Claude screens every listing by title and metadata alone, filtering out obvious non-infringers like TV remotes, smartwatches, and speakers. This narrows 170+ listings down to roughly 5 candidates worth a closer look.

170 → ~5 shortlisted
👁

AI Image Analysis

Only shortlisted candidates move to vision analysis. Claude examines actual product photos against our patented features: button layout, form factor, mounting mechanism, and wearability. Each product gets a risk level with written reasoning.

Vision AI on real product photos
📧

Email Report + One-Click Filing

Every HIGH and MEDIUM result is logged to Notion for tracking. A formatted email report follows with product images, the AI's reasoning, and a direct link to Amazon Brand Registry with patent details pre-filled.

10 seconds to file a complaint
The Smart Part

It Gets Better the More We Use It

🧠

Learns From Our Reviews

When we mark a missed product as "Should Have Flagged" in Notion, the next scan incorporates that correction. Every review makes the filter more accurate.

🎯

Tracks Known Bad Actors

Brands we've caught before are automatically flagged for deep analysis no matter what they put in their title. They can't sneak back without us knowing.

🛡

Can't Be Fooled

Some sellers add "NOT Wearable" to their titles to dodge detection. The system ignores self-labeling entirely and evaluates the actual product form factor from images.

📬

Only Emails When It Matters

No new findings and no pending reviews? No email. Reports only go out when there's something actionable, or when flagged items are still awaiting review.

How It Got Built

Two Weeks of Figuring It Out

There were a few false starts along the way. Here's how it actually went:

Mar 11-14

Tried building it no-code. Ran it 7 times, got 7 errors. Amazon's data was too messy. Scrapped it and switched to Python.

Mar 14-18

Applied for Amazon's API access. AWS credentials, developer app registration, then waited for Amazon to approve. Approved March 18.

Mar 18

Every API call returned "Access Denied." Hours of debugging. The problem? A single missing letter in a 13-character ID. One letter. That kind of day.

Mar 18-20

First working version. Could search Amazon and analyze results, but pretty limited. One keyword, 10 results, no images, no memory.

Mar 20

Emails going straight to spam. First email provider killed their free plan. Switched providers, then had to set up domain verification before emails stopped getting flagged.

Mar 20-21

Tuned the AI's judgment. It was flagging TV remotes and TikTok rings as infringers. Rewrote the detection rules to match our actual patents.

Mar 21

Full code audit found 10 problems. Only seeing page 1 of results, no rate limiting, both patents using the same rules. Rebuilt everything. Added image analysis. Cut costs 90% by screening with text first.

Mar 21

Deployed to production. First real catch on day one.

Mar 27

A known infringer slipped through by putting "NOT Wearable" in their title. Added anti-evasion rules and a brand watchlist. Caught on the next scan.

Mar 27

Upgraded report formatting. Full image cards for MEDIUM risk items, thumbnails in pending review section. Replaced the generic complaint button with a direct Brand Registry link and a pre-written complaint template.

Under the Hood

The Stack

Python Amazon SP-API Claude AI Claude Vision Notion API Resend Railway GitHub AWS

The whole thing is one Python file. No frameworks, no complicated infrastructure. It deploys automatically every time we push code to GitHub.

The cost trick is the two-phase approach: we use a cheap, fast AI model to screen all 170+ listings by text alone. Only the ~5 that look suspicious get sent to Claude Vision for actual image analysis. So we're not burning tokens analyzing photos of TV remotes and Bluetooth speakers. Vision only runs when it matters. This cut our AI costs by about 90%.

Bigger Picture

This Could Be a Service

The companies that do this professionally charge a lot of money. Red Points, BrandShield, Corsearch, MarkMonitor are the big names in brand protection and IP monitoring. Red Points alone charges $35,000 to $70,000 per year. That's $3,000 to $6,000 a month.

Those platforms are built for Fortune 500 companies with massive patent portfolios across dozens of marketplaces and countries. They're enterprise software with enterprise pricing.
