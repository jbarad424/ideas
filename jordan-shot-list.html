CB2 Reference Photo Shot List

For Jordan — April 2026 — Takes ~20 minutes total

What This Is For

We're using AI to generate marketing photos of the CB2 on people doing sports. The AI needs a reference photo showing what the CB2 actually looks like. The current reference photos have two real problems we need you to fix:

  1. The button symbols on our existing Hunter unit are faded — the AI keeps drawing garbled symbols
  2. The frosted plastic LED cover is missing on the unit we photographed before

That's it. The AI handles helmets, gloves, jackets, and scenes from the prompt. You just need to give it a clean look at the product.

KEY RULE: These are NOT action shots. Don't gear up. Don't go to a mountain. Just clean photos of the CB2 in good light. Total time: ~20 minutes.

The 4 Shots

MUST HAVE HUNTER Shot 1 — Product Close-Up

Studio-style product shot · ~3 angles · 2 minutes

Setup: Updated Hunter unit on a clean neutral surface (white paper, gray cloth, wood table — anything plain). Soft window light or a cheap softbox.

What must be visible and sharp:

  • All 5 button symbols (+, ▷▷, play/pause, ◁◁, −) — bright outlines, no fading
  • The frosted plastic LED cover above the + button — must be intact
  • The velcro strap with the Chubby Buttons logo

Angles: Straight-on top-down · slight 3/4 angle · close-up on the button face

MUST HAVE PATRIOT Shot 2 — Product Close-Up

Same as Shot 1, Patriot unit · ~3 angles · 2 minutes

Identical setup to Shot 1, but with the Patriot unit. We currently have zero clean Patriot reference photos, so this one is critical.

MUST HAVE HUNTER Shot 3 — On Forearm Over Jacket Sleeve

Casual outdoor or well-lit indoor · 3 angles · 5 minutes

Setup: You (or anyone) wearing any jacket with sleeves — leather, fleece, denim, ski jacket, doesn't matter. Hunter unit strapped on the LEFT forearm OVER the sleeve, halfway between wrist and elbow.

Why this shot: Our current main reference shows people in T-shirts. The AI usually overrides that and draws a jacket from the prompt, but not always. A "remote on jacketed forearm" reference will boost reliability.

Photographer takes the photo — don't shoot down at your own arm.

What must be visible:

  • CB2 clearly on top of the jacket sleeve (NOT on bare skin, NOT with sleeve pushed up)
  • All 5 button symbols readable
  • LED frosted plastic intact
  • Strap with logo visible
  • Waist-up framing — show arm + upper body

Angles: Front-facing (photographer in front of you) · 3/4 from the side · close-up on the forearm

MUST HAVE PATRIOT Shot 4 — On Forearm Over Jacket Sleeve

Same as Shot 3, Patriot unit · 3 angles · 5 minutes

Identical setup to Shot 3, Patriot unit. Same jacket is fine (or different — doesn't matter).

Optional Bonus Shots (If You Have Time)

BONUS TRON Shot 5 — On Forearm Over Jacket Sleeve

Same setup as Shot 3 · 3 minutes

We have 45 great Tron motorcycle photos already, but a clean Tron-on-jacket reference would round out the set. Skip this if you're short on time.

BONUS Shot 6 — Right Arm Versions

Same as Shots 3-4, but on the RIGHT forearm · 3 minutes

The button orientation flips on the right arm. Having a right-arm reference for each colorway helps the AI render that correctly.

Workflow

  1. Hunter close-up (Shot 1) — 2 min
  2. Swap to Patriot, repeat close-up (Shot 2) — 2 min
  3. Put on a jacket, Hunter on left forearm (Shot 3) — 5 min
  4. Swap to Patriot, same jacket, same pose (Shot 4) — 5 min
  5. Bonus shots if time allows — 6 min
USE UPDATED UNITS ONLY: The point of this whole shoot is to fix the faded-symbol problem. Make sure the units have fresh symbol paint with bright outlines and the frosted LED plastic circle intact. If only one Hunter unit has fresh symbols, use that one.

What We Already Have (Don't Re-Shoot)

  • 45 Tron motorcycle photos (Mike Moto Photoshoot) — covers Tron + moto entirely
  • J&Mike Dual reference (current production reference, casual T-shirts) — still works for most generations
  • Tahoe MTB shot (Tron) — usable as MTB reference

Why So Few Photos?

Earlier in this project we were planning a much bigger shoot — helmets, gloves, full sport gear, multiple sports. Then we figured out that the AI handles all of that from the text prompt. The only things the AI can't figure out are:

  1. What the CB2 button symbols actually look like (needs a clean product shot)
  2. Whether the device sits ON TOP of a sleeve vs. on bare skin (needs a "remote-over-jacket" reference)

Those are the only two things this shoot needs to solve. Hence: 4 photos, ~20 minutes.
